Roasting Process

  • Sourcing High-Quality Beans: High-quality Arabica beans sourced from the Nepalese hills.
  • Green Bean Inspection: Inspection to ensure quality standards.
  • Preheating the Roaster: Roaster preheated to desired temperature.
  • Charging the Roaster: Loading green beans into the roaster.
  • Drying Phase: Initial drying of beans.
  • First Crack: Chemical reaction signaling flavor development.
  • Development Phase: Majority of roasting for flavor development.
  • Cooling Phase: Rapid cooling to stop the roasting process.
  • Quality Control: Inspection for color consistency and size.
  • Packaging: Airtight packaging for freshness.
  • Labeling and Branding: Application of branding reflecting organic, sustainable, and high-quality commitment.
  • Storage: Store in a cool, dark place to maintain freshness.

Coffe Roasting Temperature guide

The temperature at which coffee beans are roasted plays a crucial role in determining the roast level and flavor profile. For a medium-dark roast, the temperature typically ranges between 437°F to 446°F (225°C to 230°C).

  • Drying Phase (200°C - 205°C / 392°F - 401°F): Initial drying of green beans.
  • First Crack (205°C - 220°C / 401°F - 428°F): Beginning of flavor development.
  • Development Phase (220°C - 230°C / 428°F - 446°F): Main phase for roasting, control rate for desired roast level.
  • Cooling (230°C / 446°F and above): Rapid cooling to stop roasting.
